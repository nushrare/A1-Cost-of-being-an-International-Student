The Cost of Staying

My monthly reality as an international student in Toronto

Toronto offered me education, independence, and possibility. But behind that dream is a quieter, more relentless story: rent, groceries, transit, tuition pressure, and the constant mental arithmetic of living away from home.

Before anything else, rent takes the room.

My monthly budget begins with housing. Rent is not simply one category among many; it is the foundation that determines how much freedom is left for food, movement, self-care, school, and savings.

The doughnut chart shows how quickly one fixed cost can dominate the financial life of an international student.

Chart 2

Some costs are flexible. Others are not.

The horizontal bar chart makes the pressure easier to read. Housing and tuition-related costs sit at the top, while smaller categories such as phone bills or personal care still matter because they accumulate quietly.

This is where the story becomes personal: every smaller expense asks whether comfort, dignity, or convenience can be postponed.

What the data reveals

The cost of being an international student in Toronto is not only financial. It is emotional, social, and deeply personal. Each expense represents a decision about what to prioritize, what to delay, and what it takes to keep building a future away from home.
