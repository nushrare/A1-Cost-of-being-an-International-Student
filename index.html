Nearly $280,000 to Stay

The private cost of pursuing an education in Toronto

I arrived in Toronto from India carrying ambition, discipline, and the belief that education could become a bridge to a larger life. Four years later, the numbers reveal a more complex truth: studying abroad is not only an academic journey, but a luxury-grade financial undertaking paid through tuition, rent, currency conversion, restraint, and relentless endurance.

Closing Reflection

What this cost really represents

These figures are not merely financial. They hold distance from home, family sacrifice, repeated semesters, exchange-rate anxiety, and the private pressure to make every dollar look justified.

Being an international student means carrying two currencies at once: the Canadian dollar that pays the invoice, and the emotional currency of hope, discipline, and consequence.
