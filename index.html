A personal data story on the true cost of studying, surviving, and building a future in Toronto.

This project translates four years of international student life into a financial portrait. Behind every number is a quiet transaction: tuition paid to remain enrolled, rent paid to remain housed, groceries bought carefully, flights home priced like luxury, and a currency conversion that turns Canadian dollars into a far heavier Indian rupee reality.

Final Reflection

What the data ultimately reveals

This is not simply a student budget. It is a ledger of migration, ambition, distance, and sacrifice. The cost of being an international student is paid not only in Canadian dollars, but in family resources, exchange-rate anxiety, delayed comfort, and the pressure to make every year count.
