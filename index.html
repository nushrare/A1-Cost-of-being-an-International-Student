Nearly $280,000 to Stay

The cost of being an international student in Toronto

I came to Toronto for education, independence, and possibility. What I did not fully understand at the time was the cost of staying. Over four years, that cost has quietly accumulated into something far larger than expected.

Tuition alone takes nearly half.

The largest cost is not rent. It is tuition. Each year, tens of thousands of dollars are paid upfront just to remain enrolled.

Living costs don't look big — until they stack.

Rent, food, and transportation may seem manageable on their own. But together, they create a second layer of pressure that runs parallel to tuition.

The accumulation is what defines the experience. Small costs repeat every month, quietly shaping daily life.

What this cost really represents

These numbers are not just financial. They represent distance from home, pressure to succeed, and the weight of investment placed on the future.

Being an international student means constantly balancing ambition with constraint. The cost is not only paid in dollars, but in decisions, trade-offs, and time.
